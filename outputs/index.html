BCRI Score
0 – 100 · Higher is stronger

Numerical composite measure from 0 to 100 across architecture, entropy, exposure, and coordination. Subject to entropy degradation without active maintenance.

Resilience Rating
AAA → Floor

Standardized tiered classification from AAA to Floor. Based on score relative to the Resilience Sufficiency Threshold of BCRI 75.

Frontier Positioning
ERE = (BCRI − 25) / √CS

Relative resilience efficiency given system complexity. Peak efficiency near CS 4.5. Beyond the frontier, added complexity yields diminishing returns.

Resilience Gap Analysis
ARS · ERI · XRI · CHS

Snapshot of structural weaknesses. Identifies which pillars, entropy factors, or exposure conditions reduce the BCRI score.

Resilience Upgrade Path
Pillar · Entropy · Complexity

Pillar-specific improvements to raise BCRI score, reduce entropy risk, or improve resilience efficiency without unnecessary added complexity.

Interpretation Reference
Resilience Sufficiency Threshold

Minimum BCRI of 75 required for structural sufficiency. Architectures below this threshold are considered structurally insufficient regardless of other factors.

Resilience Efficiency Frontier

Curve representing peak resilience per unit of complexity. Peaks near CS 4.5 in the benchmark dataset. Beyond the peak, added complexity yields diminishing resilience returns.

Custodial Entropy Effect

All output values are subject to entropy degradation over time. A high initial BCRI does not guarantee sustained resilience without periodic review and succession planning.
