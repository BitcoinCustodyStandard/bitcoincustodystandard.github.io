BCS Initiative

About the Bitcoin Custody Standard Initiative

The Bitcoin Custody Standard Initiative (BCSI) is the steward of the Bitcoin Custody Standard (BCS) and its associated publications. The BCSI exists to define, maintain, and evolve a rigorous, independent framework for evaluating the structural resilience of Bitcoin custody architectures across time.

The BCSI operates as an independent initiative and is not affiliated with any custody provider, financial institution, or regulatory body. Its mandate is the long-term structural integrity of the standard — ensuring that BCS remains analytically sound, practically applicable, and independent of commercial or regulatory influence.

The BCSI's work spans three areas: normative framework development, quantitative benchmark research, and public education on custody resilience. All publications are released publicly and are freely citable under the BCS versioned publication model.

BCS Research

Research Program

The BCS research program supports the development and ongoing refinement of the Bitcoin Custody Standard through empirical analysis, structural modeling, and applied custody research. Research outputs include benchmark datasets, methodology papers, and analytical commentaries on custody risk, structural failure modes, and resilience design.

Research is conducted in support of normative framework development and is intended to provide the evidentiary and analytical basis for BCS methodology decisions, including BCRI model parameters, scoring boundaries, and conformance thresholds.

BCS Research Archive

Research publications and ongoing work related to the Bitcoin Custody Standard are published at the BCS Research Archive on Substack. The archive includes working papers, methodology notes, and commentary on custody resilience topics aligned with the BCS framework.

Stewardship & Versioning

Stewardship

Stewardship of BCS includes maintenance of normative documents, publication of supporting research, and periodic revision of the standard to ensure continued alignment with evolving custody practices, technological developments, and emerging operational risks.

Stewardship decisions are made in the interest of the long-term structural integrity of the standard and are independent of any commercial, institutional, or regulatory interest.

Versioning

BCS follows a versioned publication model. Each document release is assigned a version identifier of the form BCS-[type]-[version]. Conformance claims shall reference the specific BCS version used for assessment and shall not imply applicability to future versions without reassessment.

Version increments reflect material changes to normative content. Minor editorial corrections may be issued without a version increment and will be noted in the document record.

Review Cadence

Normative documents are reviewed annually. The next scheduled review of BCS-NS-1.1 is February 2027, and of BCS-BR-1.0 is March 2027. Reviews may be triggered earlier by significant changes in custody technology, regulatory frameworks, or structural risk conditions.

External Contributions

The BCS initiative welcomes constructive feedback and external contributions from researchers, practitioners, and industry participants — including technical review, methodological suggestions, and empirical observations related to custody resilience.

Non-Endorsement Policy

BCS publications benchmark and evaluate custody architecture design types as assessed under the BCS methodology. They do not constitute certification, regulatory guidance, or endorsement of any specific product, implementation, or service provider. Where named examples appear in BCS publications, they are cited as illustrative of the relevant architecture class and do not represent a complete enumeration or endorsement. Conformance claims based on BCS must reference the specific document version used for assessment.
