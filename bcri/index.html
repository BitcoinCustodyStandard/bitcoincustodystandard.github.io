About This Report

The BCRI Benchmark Report applies the Bitcoin Custody Resilience Index (BCRI) methodology to 14 Bitcoin custody architecture types, producing scored comparisons across structural resilience and operational complexity. It is a companion publication to the Bitcoin Custody Standard Normative Summary BCS-NS-1.1. BCS defines the normative framework; this report operationalizes that framework through structured scoring and benchmarking across 14 reference architecture classes.

Research publications and ongoing work related to the Bitcoin Custody Standard are available at the BCS Research Archive.

Abstract

The benchmark evaluates structural resilience and operational complexity across five custody zones — No Sovereignty, Single-Key, Distributed Self-Custody, Collaborative, and Institutional — and produces scored comparisons across BCRI, Complexity Score (CS), Resilience Efficiency (RE), and Excess Resilience Efficiency (ERE). The benchmark establishes that maximum structural resilience is achieved through architecture design rather than delegation; that resilience efficiency peaks at moderate operational complexity and declines as complexity increases; and that custody failure operates on two independent structural axes. Sections 2 and 3 are normative. Annex A is informative.
